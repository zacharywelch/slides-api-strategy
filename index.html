Driving Adoption

7 Levels

Today we're going to talk about 7 levels for driving adoption.
Level 1

Product

Level 1 is have a great product. It may seem obvious but you must have a solid API.

Differentiate Your API

You have to convince others to move away from the status quo, e.g. richer data, more features, better terms, or some other distinction.
Level 2

Access

Once you have a solid API you need to make it easy and fast to try with successful results. A good benchmark is can developers get up and running in less than 5 minutes?

What about authentication?

  • endpoint without authentication
  • sample partner with restricted access
The specter of authentication!

Access to You!

Let's not forget... Your team should be highly available and responsive to developer inquiries.
Is there a defined process for submitting issues to your repository on Github?
Do you have a public Slack channel in case someone wants to get in touch or discuss with the group?
Level 3

Business terms

Once you're product is successful and more and more folks are beginning to consume your services you need to begin thinking about business terms and expectations.

Be Up-Front

Be up-front with developers about things like Rate limits, e.g. Google Maps and Twitter Timeline.

Set Expectations

Level 4

Content

Documentation is a competitive advantage

Augment with videos or slideshares

Host an App Gallery

Level 5

Awareness

Don't wait for developers to come to you

  • TOI
  • Blog
  • Slack @channel
  • Lightning Talks!
Level 6

Developer Experience

Think UX First!

The processes that surround your API are just as much a part of the product as the API itself
Level 7

Community
